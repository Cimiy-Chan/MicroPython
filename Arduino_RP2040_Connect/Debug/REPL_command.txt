#Procedures to program the firmware of Arduino Nano ESP32
Use wire to short Pin B1 and GND and press the RESET button, at that time, green or blue LED will be on.
At that moment, use "Device Manager" to check which COM port is connected (in this case is COM6)
Then do the following:
esptool --chip ESP32 --port COM6 erase_flash
esptool --chip ESP32-S3 --port COM6 --baud 460800 write_flash -z 0x1000 ARDUINO_NANO_ESP32-20240602-v1.23.0.bin

After flashing and use os.uname, you can see the message (or something like this)
>>> os.uname()
(sysname='esp32', nodename='esp32', release='1.23.0', version='v1.23.0 on 2024-06-02', machine='Arduino Nano ESP32 with ESP32S3')
>>>
